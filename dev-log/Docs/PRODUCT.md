## 2. What Is Grrow?

Grrow is a structured professional development tool that maps an individual's skills across four core strengths and four career stages. It gives learners a clear picture of where they are, and leaders a shared language for growing their teams.

### The Problem It Solves

Professional development conversations are vague. "You need to work on your communication" tells someone nothing about what to do on Monday morning. Grrow makes the conversation specific — not "communication" but "Navigate in the Exploring Circle."

For leaders, it provides a consistent framework across their whole team. For learners, it gives a personal map they can actually act on. The circle is that map.

### The One Idea

The circle is the product. Not a logo, not a diagram in an explainer — the actual interface. When a user logs in, they see their circle. Segments fill based on scores. Their Keep, Focus, and Grow skillsets glow. Someone else logs in and their circle looks completely different. That's the whole product in a single glance.

Everything else — the quiz, the leader overlay, the reports — feeds into and flows out of this one visual.

## 3. The Science

Nothing in the platform is by accident. The scoring system, the labels, the four-stage progression — all of it maps to established learning science.

### The Four Stages of Competence

| Stage | Quiz Input | Response State | What it means |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unconscious incompetence | Not really | Not yet | This is new territory. |
| Conscious incompetence | Sometimes | Learning | You can do it when prompted. |
| Conscious competence | Mostly | Growing | You're building a habit. |
| Unconscious competence | Intuitively | Nailing it | You do it without thinking. |

### The 70:20:10 Insight

Research by McCall, Lombardo and Eichinger at the Centre for Creative Leadership found that roughly 70% of learning comes from on-the-job experience, 20% from others (feedback, coaching, mentoring), and 10% from formal training.

Most professional development tools focus on the 10%. Grrow is built for the 70 — the learning that happens in the work itself.

### 21st Century Learning

| Capability | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Curiosity | The engine of improvement. The restlessness that drives learning, adaptation, and innovation. Grrow uses Curiosity rather than Creativity because curiosity is the mechanism that produces creativity — and everyone believes they're curious. |
| Collaboration | Work is fundamentally social. The ability to work with, through, and alongside others is the meta-skill that multiplies everything else. |
| Communication | Ideas only have value when they land. Communication is not about talking — it's about being understood, managing up, persuading, and guiding others. |
| Critical Thinking | The ability to clarify, simplify, solve, and innovate. Making things work, not just making things. |

## 4. The Framework

### The Four Strengths

| Curiosity | Collaboration | Communication | Critical Thinking |
|---|---|---|---|
| *Make it better* | *Make it happen* | *Make it clear* | *Make it work* |

### The Four Circles — Career Stages

| Circle | Stage | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Essentials** | **Start strong. Build trust.** | Starting out is about earning trust. Showing up reliably, communicating clearly, and asking the right questions before you start. The foundations of any career. |
| **Exploring** | **Own it and improve it.** | You know how to deliver. Your focus shifts to making things better — taking ownership, seeking feedback, and actively improving. The move from reliable to valuable. |
| **Influencing** | **Shape it and ship it.** | It's no longer enough to deliver alone. You need to bring others with you. Guiding teams, shaping outcomes, influencing others, accountable for everyone's delivery. |
| **Leading** | **Set the standard. Raise the bar.** | You're not here to do the work — you open doors for others. Creating the conditions for people to succeed, grow and raise their game. Your success is their success. |

### The Skillset Grid

| Circle | Curiosity | Critical Thinking | Communication | Collaboration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Essentials | Question | Clarify | Update | Engage |
| Exploring | Challenge | Simplify | Navigate | Connect |
| Influencing | Create | Solve | Persuade | Unlock |
| Leading | Cultivate | Innovate | Guide | Inspire |

### Colour Families

The four strengths split into two families. Same family sits diagonally opposite on the circle, forming an X.

| Family | Strengths | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| **Teal** | Collaboration + Communication | Skills to work with people. Human, relational, outward-facing. |
| **Purple** | Curiosity + Critical Thinking | Skills to work with ideas. Structured, analytical, inward-facing. |

Purple pages use teal accents. Teal pages use purple accents.

### Keep / Focus / Grow

After a Snapshot is finalised, learner and leader agree on one skillset for each category.

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Keep | A genuine strength. Maintain and build on it. |
| Focus | The most important area to work on right now. One thing, done well. |
| Grow | A developing area with clear potential. Often points to the next circle up. |

KFG cards take their colour from the skillset's strength family (purple or teal), not the category.

### Circle Progression (Gap Analysis)

After completing a Snapshot, the average score across all four skillsets determines direction:

| Average Score | State | Direction | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| 51+ | Growing | Move up a circle | Ready for the next circle's challenges. |
| 50 or below | Learning | Repeat previous circle | Strengthen the foundations first. |

**The insight:** If someone is struggling at Influencing, the gap is usually in Exploring or Essentials — not Influencing itself. Foundations unlock capability above. This is the 70:20:10 principle at work.

**Button copy by direction:**

| Direction | Button Copy | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Up | "Let's look at [Circle]" | "Let's look at Exploring" |
| Repeat | "Check in on [Circle]" | "Check in on Essentials" |
| Done | "Keep. Focus. Grow." | (Leading + Growing) |

**Edge cases:**
- Essentials + Learning → Repeat Essentials ("Check in on Essentials")
- Leading + Growing → Done → "Keep. Focus. Grow."
- Second time through ANY circle → "Keep. Focus. Grow." (prevents loops, signals reflection is done)

**Learner-first KFG:** After all circles are complete, the learner pitches their Keep, Focus, and Grow selections. This creates ownership and gives the leader something to react to in the Connect conversation.

## 5. The Three User Types

| Type | What they see | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Learner | Their own circle, quiz, history, KFG | Can also be a leader in a different context. Role lives in the relationship, not on the user record. |
| Leader | Their learners' circles and results, rounds to assess, KFG picker | Sees latest results from all linked learners. Assesses the same questions independently for comparison. |
| Organisation | Custom question sets via CMS, team-wide reporting (v2) | Architected in from the start; CMS interface is v2. |

### Roles Live in Relationships

A user can be a leader in one team and a learner in another. Roles are not stored on the user record — they live in table rows. The profiles table has no role column. The `leader_id` on a learner's record establishes the link.

## 6. Tone & Voice

### Principle

Grrow is designed to feel light, but it's not lightweight.
We're confident in the science behind our framework.
And ruthless about keeping stuff simple.

### Tone

- Confident, but never corporate
- Honest, but never harsh
- Warm and fun without being fluffy or forced

### Voice — Peer Mentor

- Been there, gets it
- Talks with you, not at you
- Direct but warm
- Asks good questions, doesn't lecture
- Celebrates progress without confetti

**Not corporate coach:** no jargon, no frameworks-as-personality, no authority posturing.

**Never pumpy cheerleader:** no "You've got this!", no exclamation marks doing emotional labour.

### Voice Test

| Pumpy cheerleader | Corporate coach | Peer mentor ✓ |
|---|---|---|
| "You're crushing it! Keep going!" | "You're demonstrating strong competency progression." | "You're getting better at this. Keep it up." |
| "Time to unlock your potential!" | "Commence your development journey." | "Let's see where you're at." |
| "Amazing work! 🎉" | "Satisfactory progress recorded." | "Nice. That's a step forward." |
