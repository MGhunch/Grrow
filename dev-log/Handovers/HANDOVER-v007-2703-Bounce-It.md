## Decisions Made

- **Name: "Bounce It"** — casual, low stakes, already in Grrow vocabulary (CL-ENG-26: "share your ideas and bounce off others"). You bounce something, you don't "have a session."

- **Purpose: the capture moment** — not reflection for its own sake. Something happened at your desk, it's related to your Focus, you think about it for two minutes while it's fresh. The gap between 1:1s where things get forgotten.

- **AI character:**
  - Empathetic but not therapeutic — notices when something was hard, then moves forward
  - Has the framework but wears it lightly — three questions are the lens, not a checklist
  - Not a cheerleader (no "great insight!")
  - Not a replacement for the leader

- **The arc:**
  1. Tap Bounce It on a KFG card
  2. AI already has: skillset objective, three questions, user's score
  3. Opens with: "What's happening with this one?"
  4. User describes a situation
  5. AI asks 2–3 questions within the skillset frame
  6. Lands on: "What would you bring to your leader?"
  7. One-liner saves to notes for 1:1 prep

- **Double-duty insight:** Same 16 skillset content bodies power both:
  - Bounce It (live chat)
  - Contextual quiz notes (post-assessment, score-specific)

- **What makes this not fluff:**
  - The framework does the heavy lifting — 16 specific skillsets, observable behaviours, career stage positioning
  - Authored content is the moat — Grrow's POV made operational, not generic ChatGPT
  - Serves the real mechanism — Bounce It → captures thinking → feeds the 1:1
  - "Bounce It" framing is honest — a wall to think against, not a guru

## Example: What "contextual theory" looks like

**Navigate** (Communication × Exploring):

With authored content: "Flagging early is one of the trickier parts here — there's usually a moment where you sense it might slip but it feels too early to say. Was there a moment like that?"

The second one has a point of view. It knows something.
