## Decisions Made
- **Feature name: "Bounce It"** — casual framing, low stakes. You bounce something, you don't "have a session." Already in Grrow vocabulary (CL-ENG-26).
- **Purpose defined:** Capture moments between leader 1:1s while they're fresh. Not a replacement for the conversation — connective tissue that feeds it.
- **AI character:** Empathetic but not therapeutic. Has the framework but wears it lightly. Notices when something was hard, then moves forward. Not a cheerleader.
- **One-liner capture:** Each Bounce It session lands on a single line — "What would you bring to your leader?" Saved to notes for the 1:1 prep.
- **Double-duty insight:** Same 16 skillset content powers both Bounce It (live chat) AND contextual quiz notes (post-assessment). One authoring investment, two features.
- **The work is the content, not the AI:** Building the chat is easy. Writing 16 bodies of specific, useful Grrow thinking per skillset — that's the real product development.

## The Content Structure (per skillset)
Each skillset file needs:
- Objective (have)
- Three questions (have)
- Principles: 3–5 specific to this skill at this stage (need to write)
- Traps: what people get wrong, where they get stuck (need to write)
- What good looks like: observable behaviours, not platitudes (need to write)
- Score-specific notes: one line per state (Not yet / Learning / Growing / Nailing it) (need to write)

## What Makes This Not Fluff
- The framework does the heavy lifting — 16 specific skillsets with observable behaviours, positioned by career stage
- Authored content is the moat — Grrow's POV made operational, not generic ChatGPT
- Serves the real mechanism — Bounce It → captures thinking → feeds the 1:1
- "Bounce It" framing is honest — a wall to think against, not a guru
